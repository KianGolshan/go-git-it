## Starting an experiment

Experiments let you try ideas without any risk to your main work.

**How it works:**
When you start an experiment, Go Git It creates a completely separate copy of your project to work in. Your main line stays exactly as it was. You can experiment freely — break things, try wild ideas, delete and rewrite.

**When you're done, you have two choices:**
- ✅ **Merge** — your experiment worked! Bring the changes into your main line.
- 🗑️ **Abandon** — it didn't work out. Delete the experiment. Your main line is completely untouched.

**In the sidebar:**
While on an experiment, two extra buttons appear below the main grid. The branch badge in the header also shows the experiment name.

**Under the hood:** experiments are Git branches named `experiment/<your-name>`.
